Is the marketing profession nearing it's end?

I've been doing this for 15 years. Built agencies, made $50M+ in revenue, seen every major shift in this industry.

What's happening right now with AI is the biggest one since the internet was invented. And I think a lot of people are either in denial or just not paying attention.

So I put together a timeline of what I actually think is going to happen to agencies and marketers. Here's the short version:

VAs are already gone. 

Junior marketing staff is next. 

Generalist agencies (the "we do SEO for everyone" shops) are going to get rolled up faster than they realize, because clients will just self-serve with the same tools you're using.

What survives? Vertical specialists with proprietary data and years of industry-specific experience baked into their AI systems.

Think about it this way. If I've spent 10 years working exclusively with law firms, and my agents are trained on that data, and you're trying to compete by just pushing the same buttons I'm pushing - you lose. Every time.

The end state I'm already planning for is a fractional CMO model. One person. Full AI stack. You hire me and you get a designer, media buyer, strategist, and reporting analyst - all through me, because I've built the systems.

It sounds wild. But it's where this is going.

5 things I'd tell any marketer right now:

1. Pick a vertical and go all in. Generalists are done.
2. Document everything. Your SOPs, your processes, your first party data.
3. Run your workflows through the technology. Stop doing things by hand.
4. Build a personal brand. Reputation is how you get hired in the next decade.
5. Master these tools before your clients do. The window is still open - but not for long.
