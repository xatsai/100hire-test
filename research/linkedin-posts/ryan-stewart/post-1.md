I just off a call with a law firm paying +$20k/month to an SEO agency…they had NO CLUE what they were actually getting for that retainer.

A ton of SEO jargon. A lack of clarity on REAL performance.

Modern legal SEO is not magic. It’s not proprietary. And it’s absolutely something you can understand (and even execute) internally at your firm.

At a high level, it comes down to 3 levers:

1. Content (Depth + Intent)

You need legitimate, high-intent pages targeting what you actually want to rank for.

 • City + practice combinations
 • 3,000–5,000 words
 • Clear FAQs
 • Internal linking
 • Conversion-focused structure

Comprehensive coverage of search intent.

2. Local Authority

Your GBP is often more important than your website.

 • Consistent review acquisition (ongoing, not once a quarter)
 • Proper category selection
 • Optimized services/products
 • Real activity

In competitive metros, reviews are leverage.

3. Authority Signals (Links, PR)

 This is the hardest part. Google still values authority.

That means:

 • Local media mentions
 • Niche legal features
 • Heavy branded search
 • Legitimate placements
 • Strategic outreach

This is where most firms fall short — and where agencies justify big retainers.
But here’s the part most agency owners won’t say:

You could execute this internally for under $5k/month in hard costs if you build a system.

Content + review process + structured outreach.

That’s it.

The reason so many firms are jaded is because SEO was sold as complexity.
 It’s really just consistency.
