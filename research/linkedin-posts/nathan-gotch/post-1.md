Sorry to say it, but AI is absolutely going to wipe out mediocre SEO professionals.

For example, I'm currently playing with the Advisor tool in Rankability. 

I integrated multiple data sources: Google Analytics 4, Google Search Console, Google Business Profile, and the knowledge base. 

I also ran a full crawl of the site and fed all of that into Advisor.

Within seconds, it identified that a law firm's top-performing pages were all accident news articles generating low-intent traffic, while their high-intent commercial pages for terms like "car accident lawyer" and "personal injury lawyer" were underperforming. 

It connected their brand positioning, their trust signals, their actual search performance data, and laid out exactly where the ROI opportunity was.

That's not surface-level analysis. 

That's the kind of strategic insight that takes a senior SEO hours to piece together across multiple tools and spreadsheets.

Could a strong SEO expert deliver the same quality of analysis? Absolutely. 

But the problem is speed. It's incredibly hard for a human to compete with that kind of velocity.

When you equip AI with the right data, the right information, and the right context, it becomes very difficult to match. 

To be honest, I'm very confident in my SEO skills, but even seeing this in action is a little concerning. It's scary smart.
