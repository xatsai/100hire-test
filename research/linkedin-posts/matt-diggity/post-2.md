AI Overviews now appear on 20%+ of all US keywords.

And if you rank #1 on a SERP with an AI Overview? Your clicks drop by 34.5% on average.

You're not losing rankings. You're losing clicks to Google's own AI answers.

Here's how to get AI to cite YOUR content instead of your competitors':

1/ Write direct, extractable answers

AI doesn't read your whole page. It pulls passages.

Every paragraph needs to work as a standalone answer.

Put a bold, 50-word direct answer right below each heading.

Use the format: "[Query] is [Answer]."

Don't bury answers under intros and fluff

AI rewards clarity, not word count

2/ Increase your fact density

AI systems prioritize content packed with unique data points.

- Add original stats, case studies, and specific numbers
- Include comparison tables (LLMs love structured data)
- Build FAQ sections that answer "People Also Ask" questions
- Add definition boxes and step-by-step lists

Generic content gets ignored. Fact-dense content gets cited.

3/ Use schema markup everywhere

AI crawlers parse structured data to understand your content faster.

Article schema on every blog post
- FAQPage schema on Q&A content
- HowTo schema on tutorials
- Organization schema on your About page
- Product schema for e-commerce pages

Also: check your robots.txt. Make sure you're not blocking GPTBot, ClaudeBot, or PerplexityBot.

4/ Keep content fresh

AI engines weigh recency when selecting sources.

A guide from 2024 with no updates will lose to a 2026 article on the same topic; even if yours is better.

- Add a visible "Last updated" timestamp
- Refresh stats and examples quarterly
- Update your cornerstone content first; that's where citations come from

5/ Measure what matters

Old SEO metrics won't cut it.

Track these instead:

- AI citation frequency: how often your brand appears in AI answers
- Share of voice: your mentions vs. competitors across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity
- Citation sentiment: is AI representing your brand accurately?
- AI-referred traffic: visits from AI search (track via GA4 attribution)

Tools like Ahrefs Brand Radar now track 260M+ monthly prompts. Use them.
