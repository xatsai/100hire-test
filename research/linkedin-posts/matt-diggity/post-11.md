ChatGPT traffic is converting 31% better than organic Google traffic. 

And almost nobody is paying attention to it yet.

According to a Visibility Labs analysis of 94 seven- and eight-figure ecommerce brands throughout 2025, ChatGPT referral traffic converted at 1.81% compared to 1.39% for non-branded organic search.

That's a 31% higher conversion rate.

And it makes sense when you think about how people actually use ChatGPT to shop. 

They're narrowing down what they want BEFORE they click. By the time they land on a product page, half the decision is already made.

The growth curve is nuts too. ChatGPT visits grew 1,079% across the year, from around 1,500 sessions in January to over 18,000 in December.

Plus, revenue-per-session from ChatGPT traffic was $3.65 vs $3.30 from organic. 

Higher conversions AND higher revenue per visit.

The catch? Volume is still small. 

ChatGPT drove about $474K in revenue compared to $32.1 million from non-branded organic. That's roughly 1.5% of organic revenue.

And the real number is probably bigger than that. 

Visibility Labs flagged a major attribution gap. 

People get product recommendations from ChatGPT, then go Google the brand name and buy through that search instead. 

GA4 counts those as branded organic, not AI referrals.

So if you're running an ecommerce brand and you're not thinking about how your products show up in AI answers… you're leaving money on the table that's only going to grow.
