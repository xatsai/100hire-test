The businesses I see growing fastest in 2026 are creating LESS content, not more. But they're putting it in very different places.

There are two types of content marketing. Push and pull.

Push is Instagram, TikTok, Facebook. 

You post something, it lives in a feed for maybe 24-48 hours, and then it's basically dead. 

You have to keep feeding the machine just to stay visible.

Pull is YouTube, blogs, Pinterest. 

People are actively searching for answers. Your content gets found by the right people at the right time, and it keeps working for months or years after you hit publish.

I have clients with blog posts and videos from 2+ years ago still generating leads every single day. 

Meanwhile, their competitors are grinding out 5 Instagram posts a week wondering why nothing's converting.

Plus, most people are measuring the wrong things. Likes and followers are monopoly money. 

The only numbers that matter are leads generated and clients acquired.

If you can't trace a piece of content back to actual revenue, you need to seriously question whether that platform deserves your time.

My advice for 2026. Pick one search-based platform (YouTube or blog content). Create less but go deeper. 

Track everything with UTM links. And build an email list from day one, because that's the only audience you truly own.
