Google Search and Gemini might converge. 

Or diverge. Or get replaced by a third product nobody's seen yet. 

Google's own head of Search said this publicly this week. 

According to Search Engine Land, Liz Reid was asked whether Search and Gemini are merging. 

Her answer was essentially "maybe, maybe not, who knows."

She drew a distinction between the two right now: Search is about connecting people to the web, Gemini is more about productivity and creation. 

But she also said the lines are fluid and could go either direction.

She even floated the idea that agents could make both products obsolete and a third product takes over entirely.

What does this mean for SEO?

Stop building your entire traffic strategy around one channel. 

The businesses that will be fine are the ones showing up across Search, AI Overviews, Gemini, ChatGPT, and whatever comes next, because at this point nobody, including Google, knows which one wins.

The fundamentals still hold. 

Real authority, quality content, brand signals. 

Those travel across platforms regardless of what Google decides to do with its product lineup.
