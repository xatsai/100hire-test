AI is eating clicks. Social is stealing attention. Google rewards big brands. 

So how do you grow a site from zero in 2026? 

Here are 6 tactics that still work (tested and proven across hundreds of client sites) 👇

1/ Get listed in the right directories

Not just Google Business Profile, Yelp, and Apple Maps. I'm talking about niche-specific ones.

TripAdvisor for travel. Avvo for lawyers. Houzz for home design.

These listings tell Google AND AI systems that your brand exists and is active in your space.

Use Ahrefs' Link Intersect tool to find directories your competitors are listed in that you're not. Then go submit. 

Near 100% success rate and takes almost no effort.

2/ Build free tools

According to Ahrefs' research, AI Overviews cause clicks to the top result to drop roughly 35%. 

But when someone searches for a tool (mortgage calculator, backlink checker, unit converter), there's no AI Overview. The clicks still go to the website.

Find tool ideas using keyword research.

Look for modifiers like "calculator," "checker," or "converter" in your niche. 

You can build simple ones with ChatGPT in minutes or hire a developer for cheap.

3/ Build an email list immediately

Every platform you rely on runs on an algorithm that can change overnight. 

Your email list is the one channel you actually own.

Pair it with your free tools using lead magnets. 

A mortgage calculator? Offer a first-time buyer checklist. 

You're qualifying visitors and giving yourself a direct line back to them whenever you want.

4/ Show up on forums and Reddit

These are some of the most underrated traffic sources because your audience is already there asking questions.

Go to Ahrefs Site Explorer, enter reddit. com, pull the organic keywords report, and filter for your niche terms. 

You'll find threads pulling thousands of visits per month from Google. Post in ones with fewer comments so yours actually stands out.

Visibility compounds. One post won't change your life. Doing it consistently will.

5/ Sweat equity partnerships

Trade your skills for access to someone else's audience. 

Design graphics for a creator. Build a co-branded tool. Contribute content they can actually use.

The key is leading with value, not a pitch. 

Do your homework. Find a real gap you can fill for someone influential in your space. 

Let the relationship develop from there.

6/ Create experience-driven content

Every tactic above can be copied. The one thing nobody can steal? Your actual experience.

If you don't have anything interesting to say, it's probably because you're not doing anything interesting. 

Do something worth talking about, then tell that story across your site, email, socials, and communities.

That human layer is what makes people choose you over the 50 other sites doing the same thing.
