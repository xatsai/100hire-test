There's a growing trend in search engine optimization.

“Smaller, high-intent pages are winning the citations.” This is according to Moz.

Recent research from Promptwatch said the same thing. “Landing pages and product pages are cited the most in AI search right after listicles.”

These are what these high-intent pages look like: they target search terms where people want to purchase, or use something, or make a discovery call. There’s a call to action right at the top of the page, above the fold. The big H1 heading is the high-intent search term.

This is a far cry from what people have focused on since the inception of SEO.

Up until now, most SEO has been low-intent articles. People are waking up to the fact that high-intent pages are better. It's always been better, but people have only been learning now.
